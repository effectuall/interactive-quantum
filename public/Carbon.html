Carbon Hybridization

Carbon has 4 valence electrons. By mixing its 2s and 2p orbitals, it can form different structures:

  • sp³: 4 single bonds, tetrahedral (109.5°). Forms Diamond.
  • sp²: 3 sigma bonds + 1 delocalized pi bond, planar (120°). Forms Graphene/Graphite.
  • sp: 2 sigma bonds + 2 pi bonds, linear (180°). Forms Carbyne.
×

Allotropes of Carbon

Allotropes are different physical forms of the same element. Carbon is the king of allotropes:

  • Diamond: 3D lattice, hardest natural material.
  • Graphite: Stacked layers of graphene. Soft and conductive.
  • Graphene: One-atom thick sheet with immense strength and conductivity.
  • Nanotubes: 1D rolled graphene cylinders.
×

Future in Electronics

Silicon is reaching its physical limits. Carbon-based electronics offer:

  • Speed: High electron mobility for THz processing.
  • Flexibility: Carbon nanotubes can be used in wearable screens and sensors.
  • Efficiency: Lower heat generation than silicon transistors.
